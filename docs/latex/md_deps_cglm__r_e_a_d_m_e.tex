Highly optimized 2D$\vert$3D math library, also known as {\bfseries{Open\+GL Mathematics (glm) for {\ttfamily C}}}. {\bfseries{cglm}} provides lot of utils to help math operations to be fast and quick to write. It is community friendly, feel free to bring any issues, bugs you faced.

Struct functions generally take their parameters as {\itshape values} and {\itshape return} their results, rather than taking pointers and writing to out parameters. That means your parameters can usually be {\ttfamily const}, if you\textquotesingle{}re into that.

The types used are actually unions that allow access to the same data multiple ways. One of those ways involves anonymous structures, available since C11. M\+S\+VC also supports it for earlier C versions out of the box and G\+C\+C/\+Clang do if you enable {\ttfamily -\/fms-\/extensions}. To explicitly enable these anonymous structures, {\ttfamily \#define C\+G\+L\+M\+\_\+\+U\+S\+E\+\_\+\+A\+N\+O\+N\+Y\+M\+O\+U\+S\+\_\+\+S\+T\+R\+U\+CT} to {\ttfamily 1}, to disable them, to {\ttfamily 0}. For backward compatibility, you can also {\ttfamily \#define C\+G\+L\+M\+\_\+\+N\+O\+\_\+\+A\+N\+O\+N\+Y\+M\+O\+U\+S\+\_\+\+S\+T\+R\+U\+CT} (value is irrelevant) to disable them.
